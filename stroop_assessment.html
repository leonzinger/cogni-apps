StroopColors Effect Assessment test

This assessment uses the Stroop effect to interfere (challenge) our ability to read and retrieve information.

For Stroop “Colors” you will see a list of color words, for example: RED, BLUE, YELLOW. At first, the color of the words will match the color names.

For example, the word “BLUE” will appear in blue letters.

  • red
  • blue
  • yellow

Stroop Effects will move from color to color and ask you which color you see. Select from the menu at the bottom of the screen the correct answer.

n Stage 2, in place of the color words will be patches of different colors, once again the object is to select the correct colorfrom the options menu..

In Stage 3, the next threescreens of Stroop, the colors of the letterswill not match the colors represented by the words.

  • black
  • blue
  • yellow

The trick is to identify the color of the letters you see. For instance, you may see the word “YELLOW” spelled out in blue. When this happens, your answer should be “blue.”
